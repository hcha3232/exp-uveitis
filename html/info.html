Welcome to Uveitis Tree!

Uveitis Tree is an interactive, decision-tree-based learning tool designed to help clinicians understand and differentiate various phenotypes of uveitis.

Using a step-by-step decision-tree model, this app guides you through key clinical questions, allowing you to learn and practice diagnostic reasoning in uveitis.

How to use Uveitis Tree

  • 1. Determine the anatomical location of inflammation

    Start by identifying the primary location of inflammation based on the SUN classification:

    • Anterior uveitis
    • Intermediate uveitis
    • Posterior uveitis
    • Panuveitis

  • 2. Select a module

    Each module presents key clinical signs and symptoms relevant to that anatomic location. These are based on real-world diagnostic approaches used by uveitis specialists.

    We will continually update definitions and images to clarify difficult-to-recognise signs (e.g., stellate KP, granulomatous KP).

  • 3. Monitor the differential diagnoses

    Each module begins with an extensive list of differentials based on SUN classification. As you answer questions, some phenotypes move higher or lower in probability. The high-probability zone represents conditions where all findings align with their classical presentation. Tap on the phenotypes and check SUN classification criteria for reference.

  • 4. Always consider syphilis, sarcoidosis, and TB

    These three important aetiologies of uveitis can present with highly variable uveitis phenotypes, from localised inflammation (i.e., Tubercular anterior uveitis) to panuveitis. Their morphological appearance can mimic other phenotypes (e.g., serpiginous-like TB, placoid-like syphilis uveitis). Therefore, the three entities are always in the differential on the moderate-probability zone.

  • 5. Top Differential

    Once you reach the top differential, it means your responses align with the classical presentation of a specific phenotype. You will see:

    • Summary: A concise overview of the phenotype
    • Look for: A list of key signs and symptoms to reinforce pattern recognitions

  • 6. Optimising the learning experience

    To enhance uveitis diagnostic skills, you can:

    • Apply the decision tree to real or hypothetical cases from your clinic.
    • Start with a known uveitis phenotype and work backward to identify key differentiating factors.
    • Explore the decision tree freely to understand different pathways and presentations.
    • Focus on pattern recognition of specific clinical signs and compare them to real clinical images from cases or literature (especially useful for posterior uveitis).
